# Exercise 1:

## Scenario 1: Declining a Party Invitation Politely
1. Poor prompt: Write an email saying I can’t come to the party.
2. Good prompt: Write a short, polite email declining a friend’s party invitation. Keep the tone warm and appreciative, mention that I wish them a great time, and briefly say I’m busy that day.
3. Comparison: poor prompt: Might sound blunt: “Sorry, can’t come.", Could be too short or awkward, Lacks emotion.
               Good prompt: Sounds thoughtful and friendly, Well-balanced and natural, Adds warmth and sincerity.

## Scenario 2: Understanding Photosynthesis for a Presentation
1. For children: Explain photosynthesis to 10-year-olds using simple language and bullet points. Include a short story example of a plant making food.
2. For Adults: Explain photosynthesis clearly for adults without using heavy scientific jargon. Use bullet points and describe what happens step by step.
3. For Experts: Give a detailed explanation of photosynthesis for biology students. Include the chemical equation, light-dependent and light-independent reactions, and describe it as if for a PowerPoint slide deck.
4. Format: For children: Simple, story-like.
           For Adults: Informative and structured.
           For Experts: Technical, with equations.

## Scenario 3: Recipe Suggestions Based on Ingredients
1. Poor prompt: Give me a recipe.
2. Good prompt: Suggest 3 dinner recipes using these ingredients: chicken, spinach, garlic, and rice. I prefer high-protein meals, can’t eat dairy, and I’m a beginner cook — so please keep the instructions simple.
3.  Comparison: poor prompt: Might give random recipes, Ignores restrictions, May be too complex.
               Good prompt: Focused on your ingredients, Matches dietary needs, Adapted for beginners.


# Exercise 2:
## Scenario A: Event Planning
### Context Setup
- Event type: Company team-building
- Attendees: 20 people
- Budget: $500 total
- City / area: [Your city, e.g., Toronto]
- Date / time: [e.g., March 10, 2025]
- Goals: Improve teamwork and communication
- Accessibility: Wheelchair accessible
- Dietary restrictions: Vegetarian, nut-free
### Prompt A – Kickoff (High-Level Planning)
1. List 3 venue options and estimated costs.
2. List 6 activity ideas and per-person costs.
3. Provide a 4-hour schedule.
4. Show a budget breakdown.
5. Give a one-paragraph recommendation.

### Prompt B – Venue Comparison
From the suggested venues, generate a comparison table with details such as capacity, rental cost, pros/cons, and accessibility. Recommend the best two and explain trade-offs briefly.

### Prompt C – Activity Plan
1. A 6-step facilitator guide
2. Supplies and cost
3. Duration
4. Roles and rules
5. Accessibility adaptations

### Prompt D – Budget Table
CSV-formatted budget with columns: Item, Category, Unit Cost, Quantity, Total, Notes. Include categories like venue, food, activities, supplies, and contingency.

### Prompt E – Food Options
Generate 4 affordable food/snack plans (no-cook, light catering, DIY, beverage-only). Include total cost, prep time, and dietary notes.

### Prompt F – Day-of Runbook
Request an hour-by-hour runbook for setup, activities, and cleanup. Include backup plans and a time | task | responsible | notes table.

### Prompt G – Communication Templates
- Email invitation (formal and casual)
- RSVP tracking sheet
- Vendor inquiry email template

### Prompt H – Vendor Negotiation
Request a polite vendor email template asking for availability, inclusions, and discounts within the $500 budget.

### Final Deliverable Prompt
- Event summary and goals
- Venue and schedule
- Itemized budget
- Runbook
- Contact list
